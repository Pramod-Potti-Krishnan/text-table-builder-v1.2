Cloud Infrastructure

Pay-as-you-go model minimizes upfront capital expenditure, shifting costs to operational expenses.

Instantly scale resources up or down based on demand, ensuring optimal performance and cost efficiency.

Less direct control over underlying hardware; configuration managed via vendor-provided tools and APIs.

Shared responsibility model: vendor secures infrastructure, user secures data and applications.

Vendor handles all infrastructure maintenance, patching, and hardware upgrades, reducing internal burden.

Rapid provisioning of resources and services, enabling faster time-to-market for new initiatives.

High availability and disaster recovery built-in, leveraging geographically distributed data centers.

On-Premise Infrastructure

Requires significant upfront capital investment in hardware, software licenses, and data center facilities.

Scaling demands purchasing and installing new hardware, leading to longer lead times and higher costs.

Full control over hardware, software stack, and network configuration to meet specific requirements.

Complete internal control over all security aspects, requiring dedicated in-house expertise and resources.

Internal IT teams are fully responsible for all maintenance, upgrades, and operational management tasks.

Procurement, installation, and configuration of hardware can result in slower deployment cycles.

Requires significant investment in redundant systems and disaster recovery planning by the organization.
