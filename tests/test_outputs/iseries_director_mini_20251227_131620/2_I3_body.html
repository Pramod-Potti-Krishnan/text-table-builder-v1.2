Cloud Infrastructure

OpEx model; pay-as-you-go reduces upfront CAPEX, transforming costs to operational expenses.

Highly elastic; scale resources quickly up or down on demand to meet fluctuating business needs.

Shared responsibility; less hardware control, more focus on application and data management.

Provider-managed infrastructure security, with robust protocols and compliance certifications.

Vendor manages hardware maintenance, patching, and upgrades, significantly reducing IT burden.

Fast provisioning of resources and services, enabling rapid deployment and quicker time-to-market.

Performance varies by service tier; generally high, optimized for diverse workloads and global access.

On-Premise Infrastructure

High upfront CAPEX for hardware, software licenses, and data center facility setup and maintenance.

Limited scalability; manual hardware procurement and installation, leading to slower expansion.

Complete ownership and control over all hardware, software, and network components and data.

Full organizational responsibility for all physical and cyber security, requiring dedicated staff.

Internal IT team manages all hardware, software, patching, and infrastructure maintenance.

Slower deployment cycles due to physical hardware procurement, installation, and configuration.

Directly controlled performance, custom-optimized for specific applications with dedicated resources.
