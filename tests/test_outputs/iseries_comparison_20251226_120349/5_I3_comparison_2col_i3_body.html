Traditional Methodology

Centralized decision-making with hierarchical control.

Linear, sequential phases; rigid planning upfront.

Focus on extensive documentation and formal approvals.

Risk avoidance; slower adaptation to market changes.

Limited cross-functional collaboration and siloed work.

Measurement based on output; reactive problem-solving.

Long development cycles; delayed feedback integration.

Modern Agile Approach

Decentralized decisions empowering cross-functional teams.

Iterative development; flexible planning and continuous delivery.

Emphasis on working solutions over extensive documentation.

Proactive risk management; rapid response to market shifts.

Fosters cross-functional collaboration and transparency.

Measure outcomes and impact; proactive, data-driven insights.

Short cycles with continuous feedback for rapid iteration.
