Traditional Approach

Rigid, sequential project phases with limited flexibility once planning is complete.

Extensive up-front documentation and detailed specifications before development begins.

Change requests are often complex, costly, and time-consuming to implement mid-project.

Customer feedback is typically gathered late in the project lifecycle, post-delivery.

Hierarchical team structures with specialized roles, often leading to communication silos.

Large, infrequent product releases after long development cycles, delaying market entry.

Risks are identified and mitigated primarily during initial planning, less adaptively.

Modern Methodology

Iterative and adaptive development cycles, embracing change throughout the project.

Focus on delivering working increments frequently with just enough documentation.

Welcomes evolving requirements; changes are integrated continuously with minimal overhead.

Continuous customer collaboration and feedback loops integrated at every sprint.

Self-organizing, cross-functional teams promote collaboration and shared ownership.

Frequent, smaller releases allow for rapid market validation and continuous improvement.

Risks are continuously assessed and managed, adapting strategies as new information emerges.
