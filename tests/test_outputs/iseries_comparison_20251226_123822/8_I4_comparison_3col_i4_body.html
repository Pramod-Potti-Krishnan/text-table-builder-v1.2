Centralized Model

All team members work from a single physical location.

Fosters strong in-person collaboration and team cohesion.

Enables quick, direct communication and rapid decisions.

Simplifies management and oversight of daily operations.

Risk of limited access to diverse talent pools.

Less flexible for individual work preferences and lifestyles.

High infrastructure costs for office space and facilities.

Distributed Model

Team members work from various remote locations globally.

Access to a wider, global talent pool without relocation.

Offers high flexibility for employees, improving work-life.

Requires robust digital tools and asynchronous communication.

Potential for communication gaps and reduced team cohesion.

Challenges in fostering a unified company culture remotely.

Reduces overheads like office space and associated utilities.

Hybrid Model

Blends in-office presence with remote work flexibility.

Offers the best of both worlds: collaboration and autonomy.

Enhances employee satisfaction and talent retention rates.

Promotes work-life balance while maintaining team connection.

Demands careful planning for equitable team experiences.

Requires adaptable leadership and strong communication strategies.

Can be complex to manage logistics and technology infrastructure.
