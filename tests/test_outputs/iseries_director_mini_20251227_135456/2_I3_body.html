Cloud Infrastructure

Pay-as-you-go model reduces upfront capital expenditure; shifts to operational expense.

Rapidly scale resources up or down on demand; highly flexible to meet fluctuating needs.

Shared responsibility model; less direct control over underlying hardware and infrastructure.

Provider manages infrastructure security; shared responsibility for data and applications.

Vendor handles hardware maintenance, patching, and infrastructure updates automatically.

Quick setup and deployment of new services and applications; faster time to market.

Performance varies based on service tier and network latency; global reach for users.

On-Premise Infrastructure

Significant upfront capital investment in hardware, software, and data center facilities.

Requires manual procurement and installation for scaling; slower and less flexible.

Full control over hardware, software, and data; complete ownership of the environment.

Organization is solely responsible for all aspects of physical and cyber security.

Internal IT staff manages all maintenance, upgrades, and operational tasks directly.

Longer deployment cycles due to hardware procurement, installation, and configuration.

Direct control over hardware for optimized performance; latency dependent on local network.
