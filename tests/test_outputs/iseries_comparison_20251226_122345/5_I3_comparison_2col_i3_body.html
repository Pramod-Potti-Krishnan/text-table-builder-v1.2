Traditional Methodologies

Sequential planning with limited early stakeholder feedback.

Extensive upfront documentation; slow adaptation to evolving needs.

Centralized decision-making; hierarchical approval processes.

Siloed teams, limiting cross-functional collaboration and knowledge sharing.

Fixed scope, budget, and timeline; costly change request management.

Reactive problem-solving; identifying issues late in the development cycle.

Deliver complete product at project end; delayed value realization.

Modern Methodologies

Iterative planning, fostering continuous stakeholder feedback loops.

Lean documentation, enabling rapid adaptation to evolving requirements.

Empowered, decentralized teams; faster decision-making cycles.

Integrated, cross-functional teams; enhanced collaboration and innovation.

Adaptable scope; continuous prioritization for maximum value delivery.

Proactive risk management; early identification and mitigation strategies.

Deliver incremental value frequently; early market validation and learning.
