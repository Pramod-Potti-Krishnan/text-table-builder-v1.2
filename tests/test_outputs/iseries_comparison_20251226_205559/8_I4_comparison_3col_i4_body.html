Centralized Team Model

All team members work from a single physical location.

Ensures strong leadership control and consistent decision-making.

Facilitates direct oversight and immediate problem-solving.

Streamlines communication within the team and across departments.

May limit access to diverse talent pools and perspectives.

Risks slower innovation due to less varied input and ideas.

Vulnerable to disruptions affecting the single location.

Distributed Team Model

Team members operate from various geographic locations.

Expands access to a global talent pool, increasing diversity.

Offers significant flexibility for employees and reduced overhead.

Enhances business continuity and resilience to local issues.

Requires robust digital communication and collaboration tools.

Presents challenges in fostering team cohesion and culture.

Managing time zone differences can impact real-time collaboration.

Hybrid Team Model

Combines in-office and remote work arrangements for teams.

Balances flexibility with opportunities for in-person interaction.

Aims to leverage benefits of both centralized and distributed models.

Supports strong culture while offering work-life balance options.

Requires clear policies and equitable treatment for all staff.

Demands careful planning for meeting spaces and technology access.

Potential for creating a two-tiered employee experience if not managed.
