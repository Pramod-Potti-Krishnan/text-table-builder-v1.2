Cloud Infrastructure

Cost Model: Primarily operational expenditure (OpEx) with pay-as-you-go pricing, reducing large upfront costs.

Scalability: High elasticity, allowing resources to be scaled up or down on demand to meet fluctuating needs.

Maintenance: The cloud provider manages all physical hardware, maintenance, and facility overhead for you.

Security: A shared responsibility model where the provider secures the cloud and the customer secures their data.

Control: Management is through APIs and web consoles, with less direct control over physical hardware.

Deployment Speed: Enables rapid provisioning and deployment of new services and infrastructure, often within minutes.

Accessibility: Resources are globally accessible via the internet, facilitating remote and distributed teams.

On-Premise Infrastructure

Cost Model: Capital expenditure (CapEx) with significant upfront investment in hardware and software licenses.

Scalability: Limited by purchased capacity; scaling requires new hardware procurement and lengthy installation.

Maintenance: Your internal IT team is fully responsible for all hardware, software updates, and repairs.

Security: Your organization has complete responsibility for all security, from physical access to data protection.

Control: Provides complete and direct control over all aspects of the hardware, software, and configurations.

Deployment Speed: Slower deployment cycles that are dependent on hardware procurement, setup, and configuration.

Accessibility: Access is typically restricted to the corporate network, often requiring a VPN for remote work.
