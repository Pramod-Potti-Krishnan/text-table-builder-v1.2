1

Pre-Boarding Essentials

  • Complete all necessary administrative paperwork and background checks prior to the start date.
  • Set up IT accounts, equipment, and workspace to ensure readiness for the new hire's arrival.
  • Distribute a welcome packet with company culture, mission, and initial day-one agenda.
2

First Day Welcome

  • Provide a warm personal welcome, facilitating introductions to key team members and manager.
  • Conduct an orientation session covering company values, policies, and immediate expectations.
  • Ensure access to all systems and tools, offering initial support for login and setup procedures.
3

Initial Integration

  • Assign an onboarding buddy to offer peer support and answer practical, day-to-day questions.
  • Schedule departmental overviews and meetings to familiarize the new hire with key functions.
  • Begin foundational training on core systems, software, and essential job-specific processes.
4

Role Acclimatization

  • Define clear performance expectations and initial goals, aligning them with departmental objectives.
  • Provide specific job-role training and opportunities for hands-on application of new skills.
  • Conduct regular check-ins with the manager to discuss progress, challenges, and provide feedback.
5

Performance & Development

  • Establish a personalized development plan outlining growth opportunities and skill enhancement areas.
  • Facilitate participation in relevant projects and initiatives to foster deeper organizational understanding.
  • Conduct a 90-day performance review to assess integration and discuss future career aspirations.
6

Ongoing Engagement

  • Promote continuous learning through workshops, mentorship programs, and advanced training options.
  • Encourage involvement in company culture, employee resource groups, and social activities.
  • Solicit feedback on the onboarding process to drive continuous improvement and enhance future experiences.
