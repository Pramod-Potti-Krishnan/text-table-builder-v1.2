Cloud Infrastructure

Cost Model: Utilizes an operational expenditure (OpEx) model with pay-as-you-go pricing for services used.

Scalability: Offers high elasticity, allowing for rapid scaling of resources up or down to meet demand.

Maintenance: The cloud provider manages all hardware, network, and underlying infrastructure maintenance.

Security: A shared responsibility model where the provider secures the cloud, and we secure what's in it.

Control: Less direct control over physical hardware and infrastructure, managed via the provider's API.

Deployment: Enables rapid provisioning and deployment of new resources, often in just a few minutes.

Accessibility: Resources are accessible from anywhere with an internet connection, promoting remote work.

On-Premise Infrastructure

Cost Model: Requires significant upfront capital expenditure (CapEx) for hardware, software, and facilities.

Scalability: Scaling requires physical hardware procurement and installation, a slower and more complex process.

Maintenance: Our internal IT team is fully responsible for all hardware, software, and infrastructure upkeep.

Security: We maintain complete responsibility for physical and network security across the entire stack.

Control: Provides complete, granular control over all aspects of the hardware, software, and data environment.

Deployment: Slower deployment cycles due to hardware procurement, configuration, and setup lead times.

Accessibility: Access is typically restricted to the corporate network, often requiring a VPN for remote users.
