Identifying Potential Threats

  • Systematically uncover and categorize all relevant internal and external risks impacting operations.
  • Utilize workshops, brainstorming sessions, and historical data to ensure comprehensive risk capture.

Assessing Risk Consequences

  • Quantify potential financial, operational, and reputational damage for each identified risk scenario.
  • Prioritize risks based on their potential severity and alignment with strategic objectives and business impact.

Probability Evaluation

  • Determine the statistical probability or qualitative frequency of each identified risk materializing.
  • Employ expert judgment, industry benchmarks, and statistical models to gauge occurrence potential accurately.

Developing Response Plans

  • Formulate specific, actionable plans to reduce risk probability or lessen potential impact if it occurs.
  • Implement robust controls, develop contingency plans, and consider insurance to manage vulnerabilities effectively.

Continuous Oversight

  • Establish regular review cycles to track risk status, assess the effectiveness of controls, and identify emerging threats.
  • Adapt strategies based on new information, evolving market conditions, and changes in the business environment.
