Cloud Infrastructure

Cost Model: Utilizes a pay-as-you-go, operational expense (OpEx) model, reducing upfront investment.

Scalability: Offers dynamic, on-demand scaling to easily adjust resources based on real-time business needs.

Maintenance: The cloud provider manages all underlying hardware, routine maintenance, and infrastructure updates.

Security: Operates on a shared responsibility model; provider secures the cloud, customer secures their data in it.

Deployment Speed: Enables rapid deployment of new services and applications, often within just minutes or hours.

Accessibility: Services are globally accessible from any location with a secure internet connection, promoting flexibility.

Control: Provides less direct control over physical hardware, with focus on service-level configurations.

On-Premise Infrastructure

Cost Model: Requires significant upfront capital expenditure (CapEx) for hardware, software, and facilities.

Scalability: Scaling involves forecasting demand and manually procuring and provisioning new physical hardware.

Maintenance: Requires a dedicated in-house IT team for ongoing server maintenance, patches, and updates.

Security: The organization retains complete control and full responsibility for all physical and network security layers.

Deployment Speed: Slower deployment cycles due to hardware procurement, setup, and internal configuration processes.

Accessibility: Access is typically restricted to the organization's internal network or requires a corporate VPN.

Control: Offers complete, granular control over all hardware, software, and infrastructure configurations.
